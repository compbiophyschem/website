Who we are

CBS Department, SNBNCBS, Kolkata.

  • Welcome to the Computational Biophysical Chemistry group at SNBNCBS, Department of Chemical and Biological Sciences. We are a multidisciplinary team merging expertise from Physics, Chemistry, and Biotechnology. Our research focuses on applying advanced computational methods rooted in statistical mechanics to solve interdisciplinary challenges in chemistry, physics, and biology. Using a molecular thermodynamics approach, we investigate molecular interactions and dynamics from small molecules like water to large biomolecular assemblies and amphiphilic molecule self-assembly. Join us as we explore the forefront of these fields and drive transformative discoveries.

Research Interest

The broad area of our research is computational molecular biophysics and theoretical physical chemistry. The primary goal is to employ state of the art computational methodologies primarily based on statistical mechanics to solve realistic problems in the interdisciplinary areas of chemistry, physics and biology. Overall, we take an approach of molecular thermodynamics to connect molecular interactions and dynamics to their function and properties. We work on a wide range of problems starting from small molecules (as small as water!) to large biomolecular assemblies and self-assembly of amphiphilic molecules.
