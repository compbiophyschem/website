What we do

Our research focuses on computational molecular biophysics and theoretical physical chemistry. We use advanced computational methodologies based on statistical mechanics to address interdisciplinary problems in chemistry, physics, and biology. Our molecular thermodynamics approach links molecular interactions and dynamics to their functions and properties. We study a broad range of issues, from small molecules like water to large biomolecular assemblies and amphiphilic molecule self-assembly.
